Aniwat Phaphuangwittayakul

Assistant Professor & Lecturer & AI Researcher

International College of Digital Innovation, Chiang Mai University

My research focuses on the intersection of computer vision, complex networks, and generative models. I am particularly interested in few-shot learning methods, knowledge discovery from large-scale data, and developing practical AI systems for real-world applications including medical imaging and transportation.

Research Interests

Exploring the frontiers of artificial intelligence and data science

Computer Vision

Image processing, object detection, visual recognition, and transformer-based architectures for automated visual understanding systems.

Complex Networks

Analysis of interconnected systems, node importance identification, skeleton network extraction, and graph-based modeling of real-world phenomena.

Knowledge Discovery

Extracting meaningful patterns and insights from large-scale datasets using advanced data mining and analytical techniques.

Generative Models

Generative adversarial networks (GANs), variational autoencoders (VAEs), and hybrid generative architectures for data synthesis.

Few-Shot Learning

Meta-learning and prototypical approaches that enable models to learn effectively from extremely limited training examples.
